Traveling nurses have always earned more than their full-time counterparts at 
hospitals. With high demand for nurses in intensive care units, especially in 
the undervaccinated South, traveling nurses can now earn more than $10,000 per 
week. Eyeing the higher pay, some regularly employed nurses are leaving their 
jobs to travel — which further exacerbates staffing challenges and pushes up 
costs. Also on today’s program: how eco-friendly products are causing aluminum 
prices to soar, retailers struggle to stock up for holiday shopping and how 
working women can apply Machiavelli’s teachings.
